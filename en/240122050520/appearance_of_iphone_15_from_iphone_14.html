Titanium - for Pro models only

iPhone 15 - Plus on the left, Pro Max on the right

In terms of exterior materials, the regular iPhone 15 is Aluminum, and the Pro is Titanium.

In general, titanium is heavier than aluminum but has higher strength, so it is widely used in airport materials and aerospace industries. However, since aluminum has higher thermal conductivity than titanium, the regular iPhone 15 may be better in terms of heat control.

Color - Regular model is prettier

The regular iPhone 15 offers five colors, more than the Pro lineup. Pink, yellow, green, blue and black. The variety of colors appears to be targeting general customers.

iPhone 15 - Regular

The iPhone 15 Pro is available in Natural, the natural color of titanium, as well as blue, white, and black. The color of the iPhone 15 Pro appears to be targeting professionals or high-end customers as it is heavier than the regular model.

iPhone 14 - Pro

Action button - something simpler might be better

The iPhone 15 Pro supports an action button on the side of the body, which can be assigned to the userâ€™s desired function. Assignable functions include silent mode, flashlight, shortcut, start voice memo, accessibility, launch camera, and focus mode.

The regular iPhone 15 only comes with Ring/Silent switch. Of course, if there is a configurable button, there are many possibilities for use, but if you are aiming for the simplicity of Steve Jobs in the early days of the iPhone, having only a Ring/Slient switch may not be a bad choice.

iPhone 15 - Side Button - Pro on the left, regular on the right

Display - too bad to distinguish between grades

The display size is the same as the previous iPhone 14. The iPhone 15 regular and Pro are 6.1 inches, and the Plus and Pro Max are 6.7 inches.

However, the resolution has increased slightly compared to the iPhone 14. Regular and Pro have the same size and resolution, and Plus and Pro Max have the same size.

Appleâ€™s strategy of dividing grades between models still remains. Only the Pro and Pro Max support adaptive refresh rate of 120Hz and always-on display. Always-On Display is a function that displays the date, time, remaining battery level, notifications, etc. on the screen even when the screen is turned off. Adaptive refresh rate 120Hz promotes the number of screen drawings per second from 60 to 120, providing smoother screen playback.

The lack of 120Hz support for the regular model is quite understandable. However, the non-support of the Always-On display function seems to be too harsh a classification, as even the entry-level model of Samsung Galaxy supports it.

Dynamic Island - Finally, regular model is also supported

iPhone 15 - Dynamic Island

In the case of the regular model, the existing iPhone 14 model was in the form of a notch with a camera hole attached to the display. However, the regular model of the iPhone 15 has a punch hole with a camera hole between the displays.

Camera Hole Design - Between iPhone 14 Regular and iPhone 15 Regular

Finally, regular model users can feel the same dynamic island sensibility as Pro model users.
